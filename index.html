A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the "Missile Man of India," was a distinguished scientist, visionary leader, and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam rose from humble beginnings to become one of India's most beloved and respected figures

Kalam's illustrious career was marked by his contributions to India's defense and space programs. As a scientist and engineer, he played a pivotal role in the development of India's indigenous missile technology, including the successful test-firing of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. His leadership and vision propelled India into the league of nations with advanced missile capabilities.


Beyond his scientific achievements, Kalam was admired for his humility, integrity, and dedication to serving the nation. He was a passionate advocate for education, particularly among India's youth, and tirelessly worked to inspire and empower the next generation of scientists and leaders. His speeches and interactions with students were marked by his warmth, wisdom, and infectious enthusiasm for learning.

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the President of India, serving a term that earned him widespread admiration and affection from people across the country. During his presidency, he continued to champion the causes of education, innovation, and social development, embodying the values of unity, integrity, and progress.

Even after his tenure as President, Kalam remained actively engaged in various educational and humanitarian initiatives. He authored several books, including the widely acclaimed "Wings of Fire," which chronicles his life and journey from a small town to the pinnacle of scientific achievement. On July 27, 2015, Kalam breathed his last while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His passing was mourned by people from all walks of life, and he left behind a legacy that continues to inspire millions around the world. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam will always be remembered as a beacon of hope, a role model for aspiring scientists and leaders, and a true patriot who dedicated his life to the service of his country and humanity.
